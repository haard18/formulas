Philosophy: We Prioritize Winners

If someone is consistently winning, we care less about their risk style. The scoring reflects this philosophy with high skill weight (50%) and lower risk weight (15%). Aggressive but winning traders aren't penalized heavily.

Composite Score

Your Overall Grade (0-100)

Think of it like a GPA:

  • 90-100: Elite trader, definitely copy
  • 70-89: Strong trader, likely profitable
  • 50-69: Average, proceed with caution
  • Below 50: Avoid copying
Mathematical Formula: Composite Score = (0.50 × Skill) + (0.15 × Risk) + (0.15 × Bot) + (0.20 × Legitimacy) Example: • Skill: 85 • Risk: 60 • Bot: 95 • Legitimacy: 70 Composite = (0.50 × 85) + (0.15 × 60) + (0.15 × 95) + (0.20 × 70) = 42.5 + 9.0 + 14.25 + 14.0 = 79.75 → 80

Skill Score (50% Weight)

What it measures: How accurately you predict market outcomes

A. Brier Score (25% of Skill)

Layman: Did your predictions match reality?

• Perfect prediction (bought Yes at 10¢, it won) = 100 points

• Bad prediction (bought Yes at 90¢, it lost) = 0 points

Brier Score = 1 - Average((prediction - outcome)²) Where: • prediction = the price you paid (e.g., 0.75 for 75¢) • outcome = 1 if your side won, 0 if it lost Example: Trade 1: Bought Yes at 60¢ (0.60), market resolved Yes (1) → (0.60 - 1)² = 0.16 Trade 2: Bought No at 30¢ (0.70 implied), market resolved No (1) → (0.70 - 1)² = 0.09 Average Brier = (0.16 + 0.09) / 2 = 0.125 Brier Score = (1 - 0.125) × 100 = 87.5

B. Win Rate (35% of Skill)

Layman: What percentage of your bets won?

Win Rate = (Number of Winning Trades / Total Trades) × 100 Example: 10 trades total 7 winners, 3 losers Win Rate = (7 / 10) × 100 = 70% Why it's important: Simple but powerful. Consistent winners are valuable.

C. Edge Consistency (20% of Skill)

Layman: Do you consistently find good prices, or just get lucky sometimes?

Measures how often you buy at better-than-50/50 odds. Consistent edge = buying undervalued outcomes repeatedly.

D. Market Difficulty (10% of Skill)

Layman: Bonus points for predicting hard markets (not just obvious ones).

Markets near 50/50 are harder to predict than 90/10 blowouts.

E. Early Entry (10% of Skill)

Layman: Did you spot the opportunity before everyone else?

Bonus for entering when market is <70% resolved. Penalty for last-minute entries (info advantage concern).

Final Skill Score Calculation: Skill Score = (0.25 × Brier) + (0.35 × Win_Rate) + (0.20 × Edge_Consistency) + (0.10 × Difficulty) + (0.10 × Early_Entry)

Risk Score (15% Weight)

What it measures: How well you manage your bankroll

A. Sharpe Ratio (30% of Risk)

Layman: Are your returns consistent, or all over the place?

High returns with low volatility = good. High returns with wild swings = risky.

Sharpe Ratio = Mean(Returns) / StdDev(Returns) Sharpe Score = Min(100, (Sharpe / 2.0) × 100) Interpretation: • Sharpe > 2.0 = Excellent (consistent profits) • Sharpe = 1.0 = Good • Sharpe < 0.5 = Poor (erratic)

B. Maximum Drawdown (30% of Risk)

Layman: What's the worst losing streak you had?

Tracks your cumulative P&L and finds the biggest peak-to-trough drop.

Interpretation: • < 10% drawdown = Excellent (score 66+) • 10-20% = Good • > 30% = Poor (blew up account)

C. Kelly Criterion (25% of Risk)

Layman: Are your bet sizes appropriate for your edge?

Kelly formula calculates optimal bet size. We penalize both overbetting (too risky) and underbetting (too timid).

D. Position Concentration (15% of Risk)

Layman: Do you put all your eggs in one basket?

Diversification reduces risk. We reward spreading bets across multiple markets.

Bot Detection (15% Weight)

What it measures: Human vs Automated Trading Patterns

We look for patterns that distinguish human traders from bots:

  • Trading frequency: Bots trade thousands of times per day
  • Hold duration: Bots flip positions in minutes/seconds
  • Session patterns: Humans take breaks, bots trade 24/7
  • Timing precision: Bots respond to events in milliseconds

Legitimacy (20% Weight)

What it measures: Is their track record trustworthy?

A. Account Age (40% of Legitimacy)

New accounts are risky. Established accounts have proven themselves over time.

• 180+ days = Veteran (100 points)

• 60-180 days = Established (70 points)

• < 60 days = Newcomer (penalty)

B. Sample Size (30% of Legitimacy)

More trades = more reliable statistics.

• 100+ markets = High confidence

• 50-100 markets = Moderate confidence

• < 20 markets = Low sample

C. Timing Patterns (30% of Legitimacy)

Last-minute trades with high win rates suggest insider information.

We penalize traders who consistently enter right before resolution with suspiciously high accuracy.

Philosophy & Design

Why We Prioritize Skill Over Risk

Traditional finance preaches "risk-adjusted returns." But prediction markets are different:

  • Inefficient markets: Mispriced opportunities exist
  • Kelly betting: Aggressive sizing is optimal when you have edge
  • Short timeframes: Drawdowns are temporary, skill persists

Our Philosophy: If someone wins 75% of the time, we don't care if they're aggressive. We care if they WIN.

Why We're Lenient on New Accounts

Old scoring logic penalized new accounts too harshly. Elite traders with 85 skill scores were classified as AVOID just for being new.

New Approach:

  • High skill (≥85) + new account = ALPHA_RISKY (not AVOID)
  • Let users decide if they trust limited sample sizes
  • Yellow flags warn, but don't block

Core Principles

  • Transparency over complexity: No black-box ML
  • Prioritize winners: High skill weight (50%)
  • Be lenient on risk: Aggressive ≠ bad if winning
  • Give newcomers a chance: Yellow flags, not red

FAQ

Q: Why don't you use machine learning?

A: Transparency. Every score is explainable. You can see WHY someone got 82 vs 75. ML models are black boxes.

Q: Can I game the system?

A: We've tried. The bot detection catches most manipulation. And if you have high skill legitimately, you'll score well anyway.

Q: What if I'm a new trader with no history?

A: Make 20+ good trades over 30+ days. If you maintain 70%+ win rate, you'll hit WATCHLIST. Prove yourself for 60 days and 50 markets to reach ALPHA.

Q: What's a "good" win rate?

A: 60%+ is very good (equivalent to 70+ skill score). 70%+ is elite. 50-60% is average.
